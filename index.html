About Me

I'm a first-year Mechanical Engineering student at MITAOE, Pune. While most of my batch is still finding their footing, I've been building practical tools aimed at solving real student problems — things that actually save time, not just look good in a submission.

My first serious project was a college predictor for MHT-CET — something I built because I found the admission process needlessly confusing. I extracted cutoff data from government PDFs, structured it, and made it searchable. That experience pulled me deep into data handling and automation thinking.

On the engineering side, I'm drawn to Automation & Control Systems — the idea that you can design a system once and let it do the repetitive work. I also follow developments in Aeronautical Technologies, especially where electronics and control theory overlap with aircraft systems.

Creative Technology

What is Creative Technology?

Creative Technology is the space where design thinking, engineering, and digital tools come together to build something that didn't exist before. It's not just coding, and it's not just design — it's using whatever tools are available to solve real problems in ways that are accessible, useful, and often surprisingly simple.

In a formal sense, it combines computer science, interaction design, and systems thinking to produce solutions that are both functional and meaningful.

"For me, creative technology is about solving student-level problems using simple and accessible tools rather than complex systems."

How It Connects to My Work

⚙
Automation

Web tools that automate repetitive tasks — like extracting PDF data — mirror the logic of control systems in mechanical engineering.

◈
Web Tools

Browser-based tools are accessible by default. No installation, no learning curve — just open and use. That's engineering-level thinking applied to software.

◻
Engineering Systems

A well-built college predictor is a system — it takes inputs, processes logic, and returns outputs. Same principle as any mechanical control loop.
